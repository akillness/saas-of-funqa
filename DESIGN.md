# FunQA Editorial Refresh

FunQA should feel like an editorial discovery desk rather than a dark operator dashboard.
The reference mood is `Mria`: airy, magazine-like, calm, premium, and story-led.

## Visual direction

- Backgrounds should feel like warm paper and soft daylight, not neon glass.
- Primary surfaces should be bright, lightly elevated cards with soft borders and subtle shadows.
- Accent colors should be terracotta, ink blue, moss, and muted plum instead of cyber teal.
- Contrast should stay strong, but the interface should feel calm before it feels technical.

## Typography

- Latin display typography should feel editorial and serif-led.
- Body text should stay highly legible and modern.
- Korean text should remain clean and sans-serif for readability and rhythm.
- Headlines should be spacious, balanced, and constrained to readable line lengths.

## Layout

- Preserve FunQA's current IA: home, search, RAG lab, admin, docs.
- Home should read like a front page: hero, issue rail, feature cards, then deeper sections.
- Search should feel like an editorial research desk: strong lead-in, clear composer, ranked cards, and a quiet inspector rail.
- Cards should group metadata above or beside content instead of scattering it.

## Interaction

- Motion should be subtle and deliberate.
- Hover states should emphasize lift and hierarchy, not glow.
- Sticky controls should remain useful but visually quiet.

## Product guardrails

- Keep FunQA branding and AI-search positioning intact.
- Do not clone the reference literally.
- Preserve responsiveness and practical search usability.
